Summary

VinoShares is a startup that I have been working on outside of my job at Erie Insurance. The idea of VinoShares is to make expensive and hard to obtain wines available by the glass instead of the bottle. Often times expensive bottles are only available for purchase by the bottle. Our solution makes all bottles available by the glass, by offering shares (5oz. pours) of the wine. This is made possible when a bottle is successfully crowdsourced by the VinoShares community (meaning that 5 shares must be bought). Because of our crowdsourcing method, a bottle can be opened and consumed in one night to prevent deterioration of the product. VinoShares partners with local restaurant hosts to hold events where consumers can come and enjoy their wine shares.

Project Goal: To build a progressive web app for users to view and purchase shares to be consumed at events in their area. Basically, we had to build this platform from the ground up.

The main workflow in the application is to browse for VinoShares events, reserve shares, and purchase them. Read through the case study below to see the workflow.

Step 1 - Browse events

Users can browse events by clicking a link in the navbar or clicking 'Get started' button on the home page. The results display on the page below. In order to narrow down the results, I added a search bar, filters, and sort feature.

Step 2 - Select shares

Once a user chooses an event, they are presented with additional details and given the option of how many shares (glasses of wine) they want. As the user scrolls the tile on the right sticks to the top so the user can update their share quantity and reserve shares from any point on the page.

Step 3 - Add billing information

When the shares are reserved, we need to collect billing information. Credit cards are saved and managed in a user's Account. A new credit can be entered here and saved for future use using the 'Save credit card' checkbox.

Step 4 - Confirmation screen

The final screen in the 'Join event' workflow is the confirmation screen. This is crucial in providing feedback to the user that they have successfully reserved their shares and the payment has processed. A review of their purchase along with a confirmation number are shown. Additionally, the user is prompted to share the event with their peers in an attempt to help successfully crowdsource the event.
